Step 1 of 8

Profile Setup

Let's start with the essentials. Complete your contestant profile — this information will be used in your official bio and program booklet.

✦ Tip

Your bio should be written in third person and highlight your most notable accomplishments, your passions, and what makes you unique. Keep it elegant and confident.

Step 2 of 8

Platform Statement

Your platform is the cause or mission you will champion as Miss Miami. It should be something you're genuinely passionate about and can speak to authentically.

✦ Tip

Judges look for passion, specificity, and feasibility. Avoid vague causes — the more specific your platform, the more memorable and credible you'll be.

Step 3 of 8

Interview Preparation

The private interview is often the most important part of competition. Practice answering these common questions until your answers feel natural and confident.

✦ Tip

Read your answers aloud and time yourself. The sweet spot for most answers is 60–90 seconds. Ask a friend to listen and give honest feedback.

Step 4 of 8

Evening Gown

Your evening gown presentation is a moment of elegance and confidence. Choose a gown that reflects your personality while meeting competition standards.

✦ Gown Guidelines

Floor-length gowns are required. Avoid overly revealing cuts. Choose a color that photographs well under stage lighting — rich jewel tones and classic neutrals tend to read best.

Gown is floor-length
Gown has been professionally altered to fit
Shoes are broken in and comfortable
I've practiced walking in full look
Step 5 of 8

Talent Showcase

Your talent performance is your moment to shine beyond beauty. Whether it's singing, dancing, spoken word or another skill — make it authentic and rehearsed.

✦ Tip

Choose something you can perform confidently under pressure. A simpler talent executed flawlessly beats a complex one performed nervously. Practice in front of an audience before competition day.
